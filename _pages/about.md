I am a **PhD Candidate in Computer Science & Artificial Intelligence** at the
[University of the Basque Country (UPV/EHU)](https://www.ehu.eus/en/en-home),
carrying out my research at the
[Donostia International Physics Center (DIPC)](https://dipc.ehu.es/en). My work sits at
the intersection of **deep learning** and **biomedical imaging**: I build
high-performance **computer vision** models that work in **data-scarce and
resource-constrained** settings, where large annotated datasets simply do not exist.

Most of my research uses **self-supervised learning** and **Vision Transformers** in
**PyTorch** to squeeze state-of-the-art accuracy out of limited microscopy data, and I
care as much about **deployment** as I do about accuracy — packaging models into
reproducible, **Dockerized** pipelines that biologists actually use. This work has been
published in **Nature Methods** and **Nature Machine Intelligence**, with **60+
citations**.

I enjoy bridging **academic research and real-world engineering**, and I am currently
exploring **applied ML / computer vision** roles in industry (thesis submission planned
for 2026).

**Focus areas:** self-supervised learning · Vision Transformers · segmentation &
image-to-image translation · biomedical & microscopy imaging · domain adaptation ·
deployment-ready ML (Docker, reproducible pipelines).
